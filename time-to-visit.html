Best time to visit Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka is the one of the best tourist destination in Asia. It is the pearl of the Indian Ocean. It is good to visit any time through the year. Country has lots of sunshine and warmth. You won't feel cold around Sri Lanka except the hills because the country is close to the equator daytime temperature is mostly around 30 celcius all year. The country faces two monsoon seasons throughout the year. One is the Southwest (Yala) monsoon which hits the south and southwest coasts of the country between May and August, and the Northeast (Yala) monsoon, which affects the east cost and the northeast between November and January. An inter monsoon happens in October which brings the rainfall through the whole island. The central part of the country remains cooler than the other parts which is not advicable to visit in October and November because of heavy rainfall may occurs it leades to flood and landslide. You can experience the cultural diverse of the country, coastal ares, glorious beaches, ancient ruins, biodiverse forest etc. It is the greatest place to visit any time of the year. Sri Lanka has full of festivals and holidays through the year.

Month by month guide

Visit Sri Lanka in January

January is a good time to visit central part or southwest of Sri Lanka and the weather is pleasant. Most days are sunny and dry not humid or not hot. Yala National Park is the most popular destination in January to see the widelife and Safaris etc. Cooler temerature in highlands is greater for explore the tea estates in Nuwara Eliya and Hiking in Ella. Weather is ideal for beach and outdoor activities like surfing swimming with more sunshine and less rain. Temperature ranging from 22 celcius in highlands to around 30 celcius on the coasts.

Events & Festivals

  • Duruthu Perehera is a vibrant Buddhist festival celebrating the Lord Buddha's first visit to Sri Lanka in Kalaniya Raja Maha Vihara near Colombo around early January. It features decorated elephants, drummers, devotes and dancers.
  • Thai Pongal is celebrated by Tamil communities in Sri Lanka around Mid-January. It's a harvest festival honoring the Sun God.
  • Whale Watching Mirissa can be in the southern coast in Mirissa, where you can spot blue whales and dolphins.
  • Adam's Peak(Sri Pada) Pilgrimage Season attracts thousands of people to climb the mountain to witness the sacred footprint and enjoy the sunrise views.

Visit Sri Lanka in February

The dry season continues all over February, which makes it an excellent time of year for outdoor activities. Particularly in the central, south, and west highlands, the weather is still warm and sunny. While wildlife safaris in national parks like Yala and Udawalawe give amazing opportunities to see elephants and other animals, Adam's Peak is the ideal place to go hiking. Beach enthusiasts can still enjoy the beaches of Galle and Mirissa. The hills have temperatures of 23°C, whereas the coasts have temperatures of about 32°C.

Events & Festivals

  • Independence Day is celebrated on February 4 with parades and cultural events across the island, marking Sri Lanka’s independence from British rule.
  • Nawam Perahera is a colorful procession at Gangaramaya Temple in Colombo, featuring decorated elephants, traditional drummers, and dancers.
  • Whale Watching continues to be popular in Mirissa, where you can spot blue whales and dolphins.

Visit Sri Lanka in March

Weather is dry in the south and west, with a slight increase in northern rain. It’s good to explore cultural sites in Dambulla & Sigiriya. Whale watching in Mirissa and Trincomalee. This month is quiet good to enjoy the quiet beaches.

Events & Festivals

  • Medin Poya is buddhist full moon day celebrating Buddha’s visit to his family.
  • Holi is celebrated in some Tamil communities with vibrant colors and festivities.

Visit Sri Lanka in April

Transition period, with the east coast starting to clear while the southwest prepares for monsoon rains. Best to visit east coast beaches and enjoy the traditional New Year festival.

Events & Festivals

  • Sinhala and Tamil New Year is celebrated mid-April celebrations with traditional games, cooking, and rituals.
  • Good Friday and Easter: Christian religious observances.
  • Poson Poya is celebrated for remebering the introduction of Buddhism to Sri Lanka with ceremonies at Mihintale.

Visit Sri Lanka in May

Southwest monsoon begins, but the east and north enjoy sunny, dry weather. Best to visit cultural cities like Anuradhapura and Polonnaruwa amd also Trincomalee and Passekudah beaches which is in the east coast of Sri Lanka.

Events & Festivals

  • Vesak Poya is the biggest Buddhist festival, commemorating Buddha’s birth, enlightenment, and death with lantern displays.
  • Kataragama Esala Festival is celebrated for honoring Lord Kataragama with processions and rituals.
  • Esala Perahera (Kandy) begins grand processions honoring the Sacred Tooth Relic

Visit Sri Lanka in June

Rain in the southwest, while the east and north remain dry and sunny. Best to do Surfing at Arugam Bay, one of the world’s best surf spots.

Events & Festivals

  • Kandy Esala Perahera (main processions) is honoring the Sacred Tooth Relic with grand parades.
  • Poson Poya: Celebrating the introduction of Buddhism to Sri Lanka with ceremonies at Mihintale.

Visit Sri Lanka in July

Dry and sunny in the east and north, with cooler weather in the highlands. Best for cultural tours in Jaffna or hill station visits like Ella.

Events & Festivals

  • Esala Perahera is grand processions in Kandy, honoring the Sacred Tooth Relic.
  • Vel Festival is the tamil Hindu festival featuring colorful processions in Colombo.

Visit Sri Lanka in August

Warm and dry in the north and east; cooler and dry in the highlands. Best to go for wildlife safaris in Wilpattu and snorkeling on the east coast, especially at Pigeon Island.

Events & Festivals

  • Nallur Festival is tamil Hindu celebration in Jaffna in Nallur Kandaswamy Temple with processions and rituals.
  • Esala Poya is significant Buddhist full moon day celebrated with temple visits.
  • Nawaratri is tamil Tindu festival celebrating goddess Durga.

Visit Sri Lanka in September

Inter-monsoon brings scattered rain across the island. Best for quieter travel to cultural sites like Sigiriya or Dambulla and also tea plantation visits in Nuwara Eliya.

Events & Festivals

  • Binara Poya: Observes the establishment of the Buddhist Bhikkhuni Sangha.

Visit Sri Lanka in October

Inter-monsoon continues, with moderate rain and cooler evenings. It’s good to explore highland attractions like Horton Plains. Off-season discounts on hotels and tours.

Events & Festivals

  • Deepavali (Diwali) is Hindu Festival of Lights celebrated with lamps and feasts.
  • Vap Poya is buddhist observance of Buddha’s rainy season retreat.

Visit Sri Lanka in November

Northeast monsoon begins; the southwest enjoys dry, pleasant weather. Best for scenic train journeys through the hill country and also west and south coast beaches like Bentota or Hikkaduwa..

Events & Festivals

  • Kartika Deepam is celebrated by Hindu honoring Lord Shiva.
  • Ill Poya is celbrated for Buddha’s renunciation.

Visit Sri Lanka in December

Dry season in the west, south, and highlands, wet in the northeast. Best to enjoy beach holidays in south or west coast.

Events & Festivals

  • Christmas is festive celebrations with decorations, services, and feasts celebrated by whole over the county people.
  • Unduvap Poya is celebrated as marks the arrival of the Bo tree sapling to Sri Lanka.
